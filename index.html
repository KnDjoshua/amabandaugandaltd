About Us

At Amabanda Uganda Ltd., we are dedicated to creating sustainable solutions that harness the unique capabilities of bamboo. By optimizing the entire value chain, we aim to maximize bamboo's social, economic, and environmental impact.

Our commitment lies in transforming sustainably harvested bamboo into long-lasting construction materials, biochar, and other eco-friendly products. This not only promotes environmental preservation but also contributes to local communities through job creation and innovation.

Our Processing and Carbon Cycle

At Amabanda, we follow a sustainable harvesting process that ensures bamboo plants are preserved while maintaining high productivity. By harvesting only a quarter of a bamboo plant annually, we promote a permanent forest that serves as a renewable resource.

Our processing involves:

  • Careful selection and harvesting of mature bamboo poles.
  • State-of-the-art drying and treatment to enhance durability.
  • Transformation into construction materials, fencing panels, and biochar.

The Bamboo Carbon Cycle

Bamboo plays a crucial role in carbon sequestration. Its rapid growth and dense biomass make it an excellent tool for capturing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

The carbon cycle includes:

  1. Absorption of CO2 during bamboo growth.
  2. Processing bamboo into durable materials or biochar, which locks in carbon.
  3. Releasing minimal emissions through renewable and sustainable practices.

Our commitment to sustainability ensures that the carbon stored in bamboo is preserved for long-term use, reducing overall emissions and promoting ecological balance.

Our Quality Standards

Sustainable Harvesting

Our bamboo is harvested using sustainable practices that ensure both environmental preservation and exceptional product quality. We follow a careful 4-year cycle, where only the bamboo poles that have reached four years of age are harvested. These poles have optimal strength and reduced sugar content, which makes them more resistant to pests and decay.

By selectively harvesting mature bamboo, we promote continuous growth and natural regeneration, preventing overharvesting and preserving the ecosystem. This approach ensures that our bamboo is not only durable and resilient but also environmentally sustainable.

Treatment

The bamboo undergoes a meticulous treatment process to enhance its longevity. We treat each pole using a 10% solution of boron salts, a safe preservative with limited impact on the environment, to protect against pests, fungi, and decay.

This treatment is applied through either dipping and vertical soak diffusion methods, ensuring thorough penetration of the preservative into the bamboo fibres. This process not only safeguards the structural integrity of the bamboo but also preserves its natural beauty, providing a high-quality, long-lasting product that meets the highest standards of excellence.
